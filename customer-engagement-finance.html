The Situation

Your data exists. It just doesn't arrive in time.

You have behavioral data, transaction signals, session logs. Somewhere in that stack, there is almost certainly a customer who is about to churn. The problem is that by the time you identify them, the window to act has already closed.

🔀

Fragmented tools, fragmented truth

Push notifications live in one tool. Email sits in another. In-app messaging runs through a third. Each platform has its own analytics, its own dashboard, its own version of reality. Stitching them together is a manual process that takes time you don't have.

⏱

Data that arrives too late to act on

By the time your weekly export lands in your inbox, the customer you needed to reach has already made their decision. Real-time insight and real-time action need to live in the same place. Right now, they don't.

What It Costs

Finance apps retain just 4.2% of users by Day 30. Most churn happens before you even know it started.

On average, 25% of users return on Day 1. By Day 30 that number drops to 5.7% across all apps. For finance apps specifically, retention at Day 30 sits at just 4.2%, according to Business of Apps. That is not a product problem. In most cases it is a timing problem. The user showed a signal, and nobody reached them at the right moment, on the right channel, with the right message.

Lost users rarely return. They go to a competitor whose platform caught the signal and acted on it. And meanwhile, the board is watching the retention curve and asking questions that get harder to answer every quarter.

"We're working on it" is a strategy that has a shelf life.

The real cost is not just churned users. It is the manual effort of teams working across disconnected tools, delayed decisions, and campaigns that were good ideas three days too late.

Who This Is For

Be honest with yourself before booking a call.

Netmera is not the right fit for every team. We would rather tell you that now than after a contract is signed.

Do not buy this if...

  • Your engagement strategy runs through a single channel. A point solution will serve you better and cost less.
  • Your user base is small and stable with no meaningful churn pressure. The platform is built for scale and will be underutilized at low volumes.
  • CRM decisions run entirely through IT. The speed advantage requires marketing teams to act independently.
  • You need a campaign sent tomorrow with zero onboarding. This is not a plug-and-play tool for one-off sends.

This is exactly right if...

  • You run a banking or fintech app with a significant active user base and retention is a board-level conversation.
  • You are managing push, email, and in-app through separate tools and spending real time stitching data together before every decision.
  • You need to act on customer behavior in real time, not after a weekly data export or manual segmentation exercise.
  • You operate in a regulated industry and cannot compromise on security, compliance, or deployment flexibility.
